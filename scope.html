Botium Toys: Cyber Risk Report

Improving Security Posture

Scope and Goals

Scope:

The entire security program at Botium Toys, including employee equipment, internal network, and systems.

Goals:

Assess existing assets and complete the controls and compliance checklist to determine necessary improvements for Botium Toys' security posture.

Current Assets

  • On-premises equipment for in-office business needs
  • Employee equipment (end-user devices, remote workstations, etc.)
  • Storefront products (on-site and online)
  • Management systems and software
  • Internet access and internal network
  • Data retention and storage
  • Legacy system maintenance

Risk Assessment

Risk Description:

Inadequate asset management and lack of proper controls, potentially non-compliant with U.S. and international regulations.

Control Best Practices:

Implement the NIST CSF, starting with the Identify function to manage assets effectively.

Risk Score:

8 out of 10 (high) due to lack of controls and compliance adherence.

Key Findings

  • All employees have access to internally stored data, including cardholder data and PII/SPII
  • Lack of encryption for customers' credit card information
  • Absence of access controls (least privilege and separation of duties)
  • No disaster recovery plans or critical data backups
  • Weak password policy and lack of centralized password management
  • Irregular maintenance schedule for legacy systems
  • Adequate physical security measures in place
